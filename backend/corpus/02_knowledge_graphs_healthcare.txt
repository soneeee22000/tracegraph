Title: Knowledge Graphs in Healthcare Decision Support Systems
Domain: Healthcare AI, Clinical Decision Support

Knowledge graphs have emerged as a foundational technology for healthcare AI systems that require explainable, traceable reasoning. In clinical settings, the ability to trace an AI recommendation back to its source evidence is not just a nice-to-have feature — it is a regulatory requirement under frameworks like the EU AI Act and FDA guidance on clinical decision support software.

Healthcare knowledge graphs typically model entities such as diseases, symptoms, medications, procedures, clinical trials, biomarkers, and patient demographics. Relationships capture clinical associations: a medication treats a disease, a biomarker indicates a condition, a clinical trial evaluates an intervention. When combined with retrieval-augmented generation, these graphs enable AI systems that can answer complex clinical queries like "What are the contraindications for Drug X in patients with comorbidity Y?" by traversing the graph rather than relying on pattern matching in text.

The UMLS (Unified Medical Language System) maintained by the National Library of Medicine provides a foundational ontology with over 4.4 million concepts and 16 million relationships. SNOMED CT, ICD-10, and RxNorm provide additional standardized vocabularies. Modern healthcare knowledge graphs build upon these standards while incorporating real-world evidence from electronic health records, clinical trial databases, and scientific literature.

Key challenges in healthcare GraphRAG include maintaining temporal accuracy (treatment guidelines change frequently), handling uncertainty and conflicting evidence, ensuring patient privacy during graph construction, and validating that AI-generated clinical recommendations are supported by current evidence-based guidelines.

Organizations like the Mayo Clinic, Cleveland Clinic, and Memorial Sloan Kettering have invested heavily in knowledge graph infrastructure to support clinical decision-making. Pharmaceutical companies use knowledge graphs for drug discovery, adverse event detection, and regulatory compliance documentation.
